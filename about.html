About Me

I’m a Computer Science student exploring a bit of everything, including cybersecurity, ethical hacking, web development, and game creation. I’m still figuring out which direction I want to take in tech, and I like it that way. I learn quickly, follow my curiosity, and enjoy diving into new areas just to see what I can build.

I’ve always loved technology, especially for creating art, but working in banking is where I realized how much computers can really do. It opened up a whole new world of systems, problem solving, and digital tools, and it encouraged me to pursue computer science more seriously.

My customer service background from Starbucks and U.S. Bank taught me how to communicate clearly, stay patient, and explain things in a way people understand. These skills help me every time I troubleshoot, debug code, or learn something new.

I have a growing collection of unfinished projects, prototypes, and small experiments. Each one pushed me to learn new tools, new concepts, and new ways to solve problems — and I see all of it as part of the creative process. It has taught me to be adaptable, resourceful, and comfortable learning quickly.

Right now, I’m focused on building a strong foundation: experimenting, studying, and developing real projects. I’m excited to keep growing in tech — whether that leads me into penetration testing, full-stack development, or an area I haven’t discovered yet.
